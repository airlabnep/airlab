Q1 2024 Research Updates: Progress and Milestones

By Lab DirectorMarch 31, 20244 min read

Greetings from Agri-Intelligence Research Lab

As we complete the first quarter of 2024, we're delighted to share significant progress across our research initiatives, field operations, and team development. This quarterly update highlights key achievements and upcoming work planned for the coming months.

Precision Agriculture Projects

Maize Nitrogen Management

Our collaborative project with IAAS Rampur progressed to its second season across 25 farmer fields in Chitwan district. First season results demonstrating 22% yield increase and 18% fertilizer reduction have generated strong farmer interest. This season, we've expanded to three districts (Chitwan, Parsa, Makwanpur) with 50 total participating farmers. Data collection is underway with weekly monitoring through May harvest.

HelioNet Project Launch

The HelioNet project (Helianthus computer vision network) officially launched in March, establishing collaborative research with Agriculture and Forestry University (AFU) on automated sunflower monitoring. Deep learning models are being trained on image datasets collected during the 2023 sunflower season. We expect deployment of the first version of our sunflower counting and sizing algorithm by June.

Technology Development

Drone Fleet Expansion

We've acquired two additional DJI Phantom 4 RTK drones, expanding our multispectral imaging capacity. This allows simultaneous field operations and redundancy for critical monitoring windows. Training for new pilots is ongoing, with two additional team members completing drone pilot certification this month.

Data Infrastructure

We've migrated our geospatial processing pipeline to Google Earth Engine (GEE), enabling scalable analysis of satellite time series data. This allows us to monitor vegetation development across entire districts using Sentinel-2 imagery. Initial processing of the 2023-2024 wheat season data is complete, with preliminary yield estimation models showing promising R² values of 0.82.

Publications and Presentations

This quarter, we published or submitted three peer-reviewed manuscripts:

  • "Multispectral Drone Imagery for Nitrogen Status Assessment in Maize" - accepted for publication in Precision Agriculture journal
  • "Evaluating Vegetation Indices for Crop Disease Detection in South Asian Agriculture" - under review at Remote Sensing Applications journal
  • "Farmer Adoption of Precision Agriculture Technologies in Nepal: Barriers and Enablers" - submitted to Agricultural Systems journal

Team members presented our work at two major conferences: the Geospatial Society of Nepal annual meeting, and the South Asian Regional Water and Agriculture Forum.

Training and Capacity Building

We conducted training workshops for extension workers in three districts, covering remote sensing fundamentals, vegetation index calculation, and interpretation for crop management. Over 80 extension workers participated. Additionally, we hosted two groups of undergraduate students for internships, introducing them to practical geospatial research methods.

Partnerships and Collaborations

Significant partnership developments include a formal research collaboration with Nepal Agricultural Research Council (NARC) for technology validation in their research stations, and a partnership with the Farmers' Cooperative Association of Chitwan for farmer engagement and feedback on our technologies.

Upcoming Milestones

Q2 2024 Plans

  • Release beta version of field monitoring mobile application for Android
  • Complete establishment of 100 demonstration plots across five districts
  • Conduct rice season drone monitoring surveys (June-August)
  • Finalize draft guidelines for drone-based crop monitoring in Nepalese context
  • Host farmer field school on precision agriculture principles

Thank You

We're grateful to our partner institutions, farmer collaborators, funding agencies, and team members for their continued support. The impressive progress this quarter is a testament to collective commitment to advancing geospatial science and precision agriculture in Nepal.

For more information about our projects, collaboration opportunities, or to schedule a visit to our research stations, please contact our office.
